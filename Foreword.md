# Foreword

In 2017, I became **one of the first full-time researchers in the cryptocurrency industry**. It wasn't a prestigious role because the role barely existed yet. There were no established research methodologies, no institutional frameworks, and certainly no clear path for others to follow. When I joined The Block in 2018 as one of the company's first employees, I had the opportunity to **build what would become the industry's first institutional research arm from the ground up**. Over the following years, **we scaled to a team of more than 40 researchers across five specialized departments**, and The Block evolved into the go-to media company for institutional readers seeking serious crypto analysis.

Throughout that journey, I faced a persistent challenge that ultimately inspired this book: how do you quickly bring talented people up to speed in an industry that moves this fast? **We received over 10,000 applications for research positions over the years.** We looked for candidates with insatiable intellectual curiosity and a near-obsessive desire to learn, but we also needed people who already possessed a solid baseline of knowledge. The problem was simple: **I had no single resource to recommend.**

YouTube and Twitter overflow with promotional content and biased takes. The truly high-quality research is either locked behind paywalls or scattered across hundreds of different sources. Academic papers are too theoretical. Podcasts assume too much prior knowledge. Technical documentation is comprehensive but intimidating. **Every resource I could point someone toward had significant limitations**, whether in accessibility, completeness, objectivity, or approachability.

**This book exists because I wish someone else had written it first, but nobody did.** Now that I've had the time to finally tackle this project, I realized it could serve as a genuine contribution to the industry. It's the resource I would have given to every promising candidate who applied to our research team. It's the foundation I would have wanted when I started in 2017.

What makes this book different isn't just its comprehensiveness, though at over 200,000 words covering everything from Bitcoin's UTXO model to quantum-resistant cryptography, it certainly is thorough. **The real difference is the approach. This book was written for people who want to actually understand how this technology works, not just invest in it or promote it.** Every chapter is designed to build on the previous ones, creating a coherent narrative rather than a scattered collection of facts. Technical concepts are explained with both precision and clarity, assuming intellectual curiosity but not prior expertise.

Importantly, **I've made this book completely open source and freely available.** You can download it, read it, share it, and even contribute feedback to improve it. This decision reflects both practical and philosophical considerations. Practically, the industry moves too fast for traditional publishing. By the time a printed crypto book hits shelves, portions of it are already outdated. **Open source allows continuous updates as protocols evolve and new innovations emerge.** Philosophically, education shouldn't be gatekept. If the cryptocurrency industry is serious about building better financial infrastructure, we need more people who genuinely understand how it works, and paywalls only limit that understanding to those who can afford it.

**This book will not teach you how to get rich**, nor will it convince you that cryptocurrency is destined to replace traditional finance. It will, however, **give you the technical and economic foundations to form your own informed opinions.** You'll understand why Bitcoin's proof-of-work creates security through energy expenditure, how Ethereum's rollup-centric roadmap trades composability for scalability, why stablecoins require banking infrastructure despite being "decentralized," and how prediction markets can forecast elections more accurately than traditional polls. 

**The industry doesn't need more hype or more dismissive skepticism. It needs more people who can think critically** about what this technology actually does, where it creates genuine value, and where it falls short of its promises. Whether you're considering a career in crypto, building applications on blockchain infrastructure, or simply trying to understand what all the noise is about, this book provides the foundation you need.

I spent years building research teams and infrastructure at The Block because I believed that rigorous, unbiased analysis could cut through the noise and help people make better decisions. **This book represents that same commitment: a comprehensive, honest, and accessible resource for anyone who wants to truly understand cryptocurrency.** 

The industry still has much to prove, but understanding it shouldn't be this hard. This book is my attempt to change that.

**Larry Cermak**
